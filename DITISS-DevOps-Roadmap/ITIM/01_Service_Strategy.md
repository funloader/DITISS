# Session 1 : ITIL V3 ‚Äì Service Strategy 

---

## 1. Concept Overview

* **Service Strategy** is the **first phase of ITIL Service Lifecycle**
* Focuses on **designing, developing, and implementing IT Service Management as a strategic asset**
* Answers **WHAT services**, **FOR WHOM**, **HOW to compete**, and **HOW value is created**
* Ensures **alignment between IT goals and Business goals**

---

## 2. Key Definitions (MCQ-Oriented)

| Term                     | Definition (Exam-Focused)                                                          |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Service Strategy**     | Guidance on designing and implementing service management as a **strategic asset** |
| **Strategic Asset**      | Capabilities + Resources that provide competitive advantage                        |
| **Utility**              | What the service does ‚Äì *Fit for Purpose*                                          |
| **Warranty**             | How well the service performs ‚Äì *Fit for Use*                                      |
| **Value**                | Combination of **Utility + Warranty**                                              |
| **Service Portfolio**    | Complete set of services managed by a service provider                             |
| **Demand Management**    | Managing and influencing customer demand for services                              |
| **Financial Management** | Managing IT service costs, budgets, and pricing                                    |

---

## 3. Main Content (Aligned with Syllabus)

### 3.1 General Strategy (ITIL Context)

* Strategy defines:

  * **Which services to offer**
  * **Which customers to serve**
  * **How to create value**
* Successful Service Strategy ensures:

  * IT goals aligned with business goals
  * IT initiatives support business outcomes
  * Strategy assessed against business results
  * Opportunities for improvement identified

---

### 3.2 Competition and Market Space

* **Market Space** = Opportunities where services can be offered
* Strategy answers:

  * *Why should customers buy these services?*
  * *Why should they buy from us?*
* Competitive differentiation achieved via:

  * Cost efficiency
  * Reliability & availability
  * Unique service offerings
  * Better customer experience

---

### 3.3 Service Provider Types (Important for MCQs)

| Type         | Description                                               |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Type I**   | Internal Service Provider ‚Äì serves one business unit      |
| **Type II**  | Shared Service Provider ‚Äì serves multiple business units  |
| **Type III** | External Service Provider ‚Äì serves multiple organizations |

---

### 3.4 Service Management as a Strategic Asset

* Service Management provides:

  * **Capabilities** (processes, skills, knowledge)
  * **Resources** (people, tools, infrastructure)
* Enables:

  * Consistent value delivery
  * Risk optimization
  * Long-term competitive advantage
* Treated as a **business enabler**, not just IT support

---

### 3.5 Key Process Activities in Service Strategy

Service Strategy is effectively managed using **three key processes**:

1. **Demand Management**
2. **Service Portfolio Management**
3. **Financial Management**

---

### 3.6 Demand Management

**Purpose**

* Manage demand from **existing and new customers**
* Avoid:

  * Excess capacity (cost without value)
  * Insufficient capacity (poor service quality)

**Key Activities**

* Market research for new services
* Handling RFPs
* Coordination with sales & marketing
* Monitoring service usage patterns

**Key Factors**

* Excess capacity ‚Üí higher cost, lower margins
* Insufficient capacity ‚Üí stunted growth

**Tools & Techniques**

* SLAs
* Forecasting & planning
* Off-peak pricing
* Volume discounts
* Differentiated service levels

**Inputs**

* Customer feedback
* Market research
* Current service performance

---

### 3.7 Service Portfolio Management

**Purpose**

* Decide **which services to offer and maintain**
* Provides direction to Service Design

**Key Questions Answered**

* Why should customer buy the service?
* Why from us?
* Is the service viable?

**Information Maintained**

* Service name & description
* Status & criticality
* Business & IT owners
* SLA / SLR / Warranty levels
* Supporting services & dependencies
* Costs, charges, revenue
* Service metrics

---

### 3.8 Financial Management

**Purpose**

* Support **business decision-making**
* Ensure cost-effective service delivery

**Key Questions Answered**

* Which services cost the most and why?
* Are we profitable or cost-efficient?
* What is service consumption volume?
* Are services competitively priced?
* Where are inefficiencies?
* CSI priority areas?

**Focus Areas**

* Cost control
* Budgeting
* Value realization
* Risk reduction

---

### 3.9 Key Roles and Responsibilities of Staff (Service Strategy)

#### Service Owner

* Accountable for service delivery
* Primary customer contact
* Ensures service meets requirements
* Identifies improvement opportunities
* Liaison with process owners
* Reports to IT / Service Management Director

#### Process Owner

* Designs and documents process
* Defines KPIs
* Reviews performance
* Improves process efficiency
* Ensures staff training
* Inputs to CSI

#### Key Supporting Roles

* Financial Manager (IT)
* Portfolio Manager
* Demand Manager
* Strategy Manager

## 4. Important Facts / Points for MCQs

* Service Strategy is **NOT operational**
* Value = Utility + Warranty
* Demand Management influences **financial decisions**
* Portfolio Management decides **what services exist**
* Financial Management supports **CSI prioritization**
* Service Strategy focuses on **long-term value**
