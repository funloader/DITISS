## 1. Concept Overview

* **NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)** is a **U.S. federal standards body**.
* It provides **globally accepted cybersecurity, privacy, and risk management frameworks**.
* **Most widely used framework for cybersecurity risk management** in exams and industry.
* **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** is the core focus for PG-DITISS MCQs.

---

## 2. Key Definitions

* **NIST:** A U.S. Department of Commerce agency that develops standards, guidelines, and best practices for technology and security.
* **Cybersecurity Framework (CSF):** A **risk-based framework** to manage and reduce cybersecurity risk.
* **Framework:** A structured set of **functions, categories, and outcomes** (not controls).
* **Profile:** Organization-specific alignment of CSF outcomes.
* **Tier:** Describes maturity level of cybersecurity risk management practices.

### üõ†Ô∏è **NIST CSF ‚Äì Five Core Functions** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

The **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** is structured around **five core functions** that represent the **lifecycle of cybersecurity risk management**. These functions provide a **high-level, strategic view** of how organizations manage cybersecurity risk.

| **CSF Function**  | **Purpose (One-Line for MCQs)**                                        |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Identify (ID)** | Understand organizational environment, assets, risks, and dependencies |
| **Protect (PR)**  | Implement safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services           |
| **Detect (DE)**   | Identify the occurrence of cybersecurity events                        |
| **Respond (RS)**  | Take action to contain and mitigate cybersecurity incidents            |
| **Recover (RC)**  | Restore services and improve resilience after incidents                |

---

### üìå **Key Exam Notes (Very Important)**

* The **5 CSF Functions** apply to **all organizations**, regardless of size or sector.
* Functions are:

  * **Sequential in concept** (Identify ‚Üí Protect ‚Üí Detect ‚Üí Respond ‚Üí Recover)
  * **Not strictly linear** in real-world implementation.
* Each function contains:

  * **Categories** ‚Üí **Subcategories** ‚Üí **Informative References**
* These functions form the **foundation of the CSF Core**.

## 4. Important Facts / Points for MCQs

* NIST CSF is **risk-based**, not control-based.
* CSF focuses on **outcomes**, not specific technologies.
* CSF is applicable to **all organizations** (size & sector neutral).
* CSF supports **enterprise risk management**, not only IT risk.
* CSF 2.0 emphasizes integration with **financial, privacy, supply chain, and reputational risks**.
