# Session 10 : Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA): Regulations, Titles & Rules

---

## 1. Concept Overview

* **HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)** is a **US federal law** enacted to:

  * Ensure **health insurance portability**
  * Protect **patient health information (PHI)**
* Establishes **national standards** for:

  * Privacy
  * Security
  * Breach notification
  * Enforcement of health data protection
* Applies mainly to **Covered Entities (CEs)** and **Business Associates (BAs)**

üìÖ **Enacted:** **21 August 1996**

---

## 2. Key Definitions (High MCQ Priority)

| Term                                   | Definition                                                                            |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **PHI (Protected Health Information)** | Any individually identifiable health information (name, diagnosis, SSN, photos, etc.) |
| **ePHI**                               | PHI that is created, stored, transmitted, or maintained electronically                |
| **Covered Entity (CE)**                | Organizations that directly handle PHI (Doctors, Hospitals, Health Insurers)          |
| **Business Associate (BA)**            | Third parties handling PHI on behalf of CEs (IT vendors, billing firms, lawyers)      |
| **HHS**                                | Department of Health & Human Services                                                 |
| **OCR**                                | Office for Civil Rights (HIPAA enforcement authority)                                 |

### B. HIPAA Titles (Very Important for MCQs)

#### **Title I ‚Äì Health Insurance Portability**

* Prevents loss of insurance due to:

  * Job change
  * Illness
* Key provisions:

  * Allows for the continuation of coverage (e.g., **COBRA**)
  * Prevents group health plans from denying coverage based on **pre-existing conditions**
  * Guaranteed renewability of insurance for employers.

#### **Title II ‚Äì Administrative Simplification**

* **Most examinable title**
* Directs HHS to define standards ‚Üí **HIPAA Rules**
* Covers:

  * Privacy
  * Security
  * Breach Notification
  * Enforcement

#### **Title III ‚Äì Tax-Related Provisions**

* Medical Savings Accounts (MSAs)
* Tax deductions & tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses

#### **Title IV ‚Äì Group Health Plan Rules**

* Pre-existing condition management
* Coverage renewal rules

#### **Title V ‚Äì Revenue Offsets**

* Miscellaneous provisions related to tax and revenue

### C. HIPAA Rules (Core Exam Area)

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Privacy Rule**

* Focus: **WHO can access PHI**
* Applies to:

  * Electronic
  * Paper
  * Oral PHI
* Establishes **patient rights**

### Patient Rights:

* Right to **Access** : Patients have the right to get a copy of their medical records in the form they request (if readily reproducible).
* Right to **Amend** : Patients can request corrections to their health information if they believe it is inaccurate or incomplete.
* Right to **Accounting of disclosures** : Patients can ask for a list of who their PHI has been shared with for reasons other than treatment, payment, or operations.

üìÑ **Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP)**

* Mandatory acknowledgement at first visit
* Informs patients how PHI is used

### Minimum Necessary Principle:

* A core concept of the Privacy Rule is the *Minimum Necessary* standard.
* This requires Covered Entities to make reasonable efforts to limit the use, disclosure of, and requests for PHI to the minimum amount necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.
* Example: Billing staff doesn‚Äôt need clinical history

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Security Rule**

* Focus: **HOW to protect ePHI**

| Safeguard          | Description         | Examples                                |

### Who Enforces HIPAA ?
**HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR)**

* The primary enforcer of HIPAA's Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules is the Office for Civil Rights (OCR) within the Department of Health and Human Services.

* The OCR investigates complaints, conducts compliance audits, and issues penalties for non-compliance.

### D. HITECH Act (2009)

* **Strengthened HIPAA**
* Key impacts:

  1. Increased penalties - Massively increased the penalties for non-compliance and introduced the tiered penalty structure.
  2. Made **Business Associates directly liable** liable for HIPAA compliance, not just the Covered Entity.
* Introduced public breach reporting:

  * **OCR ‚ÄúWall of Shame‚Äù** (breaches ‚â•500 individuals)

### E.HIPAA in the Modern Era

* **HIPAA remains relevant** despite technological changes
* Modern healthcare uses:

  * Telehealth
  * Cloud computing
  * Mobile & wearable devices
* Core idea remains unchanged: **HIPAA is about TRUST**
* Focus is on protecting **ePHI** in **digital and remote environments**

## 4. Important Facts / Points for MCQs

* HIPAA enacted in **1996**
* Privacy Rule ‚Üí **all PHI**
* Security Rule ‚Üí **only ePHI**
* Risk analysis = **cornerstone of compliance**
* BAs became liable after **HITECH Act**
* OCR enforces HIPAA
* Minimum Necessary applies to **use & disclosure**, not emergencies
